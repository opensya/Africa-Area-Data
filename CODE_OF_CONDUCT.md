## 1. Our Commitment

We are committed to creating an **open, welcoming, and respectful environment** for all people, regardless of their background, identity, experience, or role in the project. We want **everyone to feel safe and encouraged to contribute**.

## 2. Expected Behavior

We expect contributors, maintainers, and participants to:

* Show **respect and courtesy** toward others;
* **Listen actively** and consider ideas different from their own;
* Provide **constructive and kind feedback**;
* Follow the **project rules and best practices** when contributing;
* Report problems or conflicts in an **appropriate and professional manner**.

## 3. Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are **not tolerated**:

* Harassment, intimidation, or threats;
* Insults or discriminatory/degrading language based on origin, gender, age, religion, disability, etc.;
* Posting offensive or inappropriate content;
* Deliberately disrupting discussions or contributions.

## 4. Conduct Regarding Contributions

To maintain the **reliability and quality** of the data:

* Contributions must be **accurate, verifiable, and based on reliable sources** (e.g., national statistical offices, OCHA, UN, government sources).
* Submitted data must follow the **standardized JSON structure** defined by the project (`name`, `type`, `parent`, `code`, etc.).
* Each contribution should include **references or links to the sources** used.
* Errors or inconsistencies in the data should be **reported and corrected promptly**.
* Discussions about modifications or data validation must remain **constructive and respectful**.

## 5. Contributions and Interactions

* Be **collaborative**: respect discussions, pull requests, and issues.
* Respect **licenses and intellectual property**: only submit content you have rights to or that is permitted to be shared.
* Encourage **positive and constructive dialogue**, even in case of disagreements.

## 6. Reporting and Conflict Resolution

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior:

1. Contact a project maintainer or send an email to [your project contact email].
2. Provide clear and detailed information about the incident.
3. Reports will be handled **confidentially and seriously**.

## 7. Enforcement

Project maintainers reserve the right to **remove comments or contributions**, **reject unreliable data**, **block users**, or take other appropriate actions to maintain a **safe, respectful, and high-quality environment**.
